Everyone Is Welcome

Tonka Track & Field and Cross Country programs are open to all students, regardless of experience or fitness level. Our coaching staff is dedicated to helping each athlete reach their personal goals, whether that's competing at the state level or simply getting in shape and making friends.

We believe that running is for everyone. Our programs are designed to meet athletes where they are and help them grow both as competitors and as people. No tryouts, no cuts—just commitment, hard work, and a supportive team environment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need experience to join?
Not at all! Many of our most successful athletes started with no running experience. Our coaches work with athletes of all levels, from complete beginners to experienced runners. We'll help you develop the skills and fitness you need to succeed.
Can I participate in other sports?
Yes! Many of our athletes are multi-sport participants. Track and field especially accommodates this since it's a spring sport. For cross country (fall), we encourage you to talk with coaches about your schedule and commitments. Off-season training is flexible and optional.
What if I'm not very fast?
Speed is just one aspect of track and field! We have events for all types of athletes: distance running, sprinting, jumping, and throwing. Success in our program is measured by personal improvement, not just winning races. Every athlete competes against their own previous performance.
What's the difference between track and cross country?
Cross country is run in the fall and involves racing on trails, grass, and natural terrain, typically 5K (3.1 miles). Track and field is in the spring and takes place on a track with various events including sprints, distance, hurdles, relays, jumps, and throws. Many athletes participate in both!
How much does it cost to participate?
Program fees vary by season. Financial assistance is available for families who need it, and we have a used equipment exchange. No student is turned away due to cost. Contact the coaches for specific fee information and financial aid options.
What if I get injured?
Our coaches work closely with athletic trainers to ensure safe training and recovery. If you're injured, we'll help you with rehabilitation and a safe return to competition. Many athletes also take on team support roles while recovering, staying connected to the team.
Can I try different events in track and field?
Absolutely! The beginning of the season is a great time to try different events. Our coaches will help you find the events that best suit your strengths and interests. Many athletes compete in multiple events.
